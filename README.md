## What this project is

This repository studies a simple but underdeveloped truth:

> Central Asia was not made by rulers, conquerors, theologians, and poets alone.
> It was also made, continuously and often invisibly, by women.

This includes both:

- named women: rulers, khatuns, patrons, poetesses, dynastic actors;
- unnamed women: mothers, daughters, craftswomen, storytellers, shrine keepers, textile bearers, hosts, and continuity workers.

## Why this project matters

Official history often foregrounds war, dynasties, monumental authorship, and male sovereignty.
But civilizations survive through continuity work.

This repository focuses on women in relation to:

- memory and intergenerational transmission;
- legitimacy and dynastic continuity;
- food, hospitality, and ritual;
- sacred and shrine geographies;
- material culture and textile intelligence;
- oral tradition, naming, and social endurance.
